---
title: "I am afraid of being a nobody"
---

Consider the following statement:

"You will die a nobody (in the grand scheme of things).  In fact, chances are you ARE a nobody."

Do you agree with what I'm saying?  Truly, or just in principle?  Perhaps you actually disagree?

Personally, I am terrified of being a nobody.  What's even scarier is that I didn't notice that clearly for most of my life.
I agreed in principle, as in if somebody said that I would go into "logical thinking mode" and reply
"yes... of course, that's the sad bit", but I **think** I was actually hoping to be wrong.
Wouldn't it be wonderful?  To be special somehow?

Here is another statement to consider:

"Do you think you will do great things in your life?  Do you want to do great things in your life?  If so, why?"

I remember reading at one point that some people believe their high-point lies in their past while others think their high-point will be in their future.

Are you striving for something because that's gonna make you happier?

I used to think that the reason why I felt unhappy was because I didn't have something.  Unfortunately, even after getting that something the feeling didn't really go away.  Some things indeed were problems, and indeed they required fixing, but a lot of things I thought would make my life better actually didn't.

Does your lack of achievement bother you?  Do you fear of not living this one life you have to its full potential?
