title: "It's gonna suck sometimes"
---------

You'll feel like shit, unable to do anything.  If unlucky, you'll ignore the feeling with Youtube or any other form of distraction.  You might even think exercise is a good thing and use that as an escape.

Your ears will ring, it will seem your tinnitus has gotten worse.  First time you'll notice it you'll freak out.  Then you'll try to listen for the ringing it will be the only thing actually brining you into focus.  After some time it will be like meditation, the stronger you hear the ringing the more "in the moment" you will be.  Other times you won't hear it and you will never realise that you are not hearing it.  Only when you do, will you become a bit more aware of yourself.

You won't sleep.  You'll drink only water, exercise, try decaffenaited coffee but still nothing.  You will feel sleepy throughout the day, but in the evening, nada, after 21:00pm-22 you will turn off the lights and you will be sat there in your bed.  Maybe too warm, maybe can not find right position.  You'll get to know the feeling, that in which you are tired but not sleepy.  You will think of doing something but quickly realise that you are unable to do anything productive as you can not focus.  You might decide to watch some Disenchatment on Netflix or Ronnie O'Sullivan matches on YT.  You will eventually fall asleep at about the 1-2am mark.

Sometimes though it will be bad: thoughts will flood your head.  You will be angry, you won't be able to stop thinking about certain things.  Sometimes you will take a notepad (either pen & paper or phone) and write things down.  It will help and you will feel like your mind is a bit more clear.  Sometimes, if you are able to keep doing this (even though you don't want to do it and just fall asleep) you will start feeling sleepy again.

Other times you will try to relax.  You've done a few meditation exercises on an app called Headspace and you know that one exercise is to "scan" your body and just notice.  You do so and you always realise how your facial muscles are tense how you are frowning or how your lips are tight even though you are not realising it.  So you relax and continue the exercise, you keep at it for a few seconds but then you start thinking again without realising... you realise that and shift your attention to being relaxed and having a clear mind (simply observing your thoughts).  You do this for a few times and then realise that you need to move in bed.  This repeats for a few times until probably the 1-2am mark when you fall asleep anyway.

In the morning of course, you will feel sleepy.  It won't be impossible to get out of bed and you might even have a little routine going on where first thing you do to the bathroom, empty the bladder, and splash some cold water on your face.  It will help and you'll feel fine.

You will even have appetite.  A bit difficult getting the actual motivation to cook, but you can survive on fruits and semi-prepared meals from your local supermarket.

You won't groom as much as you'll be in a hurry and you might end up at work with not the best shave or a few rebel hairs.  But it's fine, you work in software so people don't have high expectations.  Or you are young and people again don't have high expectations (or even better, both!)

The day will go fine without incident, you'll laugh at the occasional joke.  Sometimes people will get loud and you will put on headphones; you'll think maybe that's the source of your tinnitus?  Anyway, you listen to some music, do some work.  You know socialising is good so you will ask people how they are and listen to what they say.  You might even show interest by asking follow-up questions.  You might be a bit quiet in the kitchen though or not really know what to say next in a conversation so you might get a bit of a "awkward silence" moment, but you remember those happen all the times and chances are it's only you who is felling awkward so no need to worry.  You might even feel a bit self-concious about certain things and trying to take things slow as you are not in your best mood.  Things go better or as well as you expect so you don't worry too much.  If you do, you realise that it is way too much worrying and it is probably only you that feels this way.

You leave work, you might be a bit asocial and not say goodbye to anybody; not that you wouldn't want to, but you know people sometimes leave without saying anything so you are not sure if this is OK or not.  People do seem to be focused on their work and there's plenty of people around so you wouldn't want to disturb them.

Going home you feel like you haven't called friends and family in quite a while but you are so drained that social interaction feels impossible.  You decide to call them tomorrow or another day when you will actually be well rested and have a bit more energy.  You might listen to an audiobook some music on your way home.


By the time you get home you feel hungry.  You hastily put together a meal, make some room on your desk (will be cleared later) and eat while watching some Netflix or any other series / videos.

Belly full and since that last episode ended on a cliff-hanger, you decide to watch another episode to see what happens.  That one also ends on a cliff hanger so you watch another one.  Sometimes instead of cliff-hangers Youtube recommendations might give you something very interesting and you can't stop yourself from watching it.

By now it's getting late, you have a big todo list but first you need to wash the dishes so you go and spend some time washing dishes, coming back to the room only to find a couple of mugs forgotten on the table so you take them to the kitchen and wash them as well.  Then, when returning you see your lunch box from work hasn't been washed so you take that and wash it as well.

Finally back in the room and nothing else to be washed.  Need to get some stuff done, but first cup of tea.  So you put the kettle on, wait for a few more minutes to boil and then make some tea.

By now it's getting late and you are tired.  You need to write some emails or look into some job applications, but they all require a clear head which you don't really have at this hour.  You would feel bad watching another episode of Better Call Saul so you decide that you can exercise a bit using your Powerball while watching it (at least you are not doing nothing).

Now it's time for bed, but previous episode ended on a cliff-hanger again so you watch the next one (only a couple more left from the first season).  Mid way through there is a long cut between chapters and you decide to call it a night - you noticed episodes generally end of cliff hangers so maybe it's good to stop now before the end of the episode.  You brush your teeth, rinse with mouthwash and head to bed - would've been nice to have a shower but it's too late you can do it tomorrow.

You remember you had trouble sleeping last night so you open a window to let some fresh air in.  You get in bed and you remember what you read in "The subtle art of not giving a fuck": life sometimes sucks.  Sometimes a bit more: you'll remember at one point you wanted to cry but were unable to.  It sucks, but it's nothing unusual.  That doesn't mean that it *should* suck, no.  It's more like poker: you get good hands and you get bad hands, but that doesn't mean that if you lose a good hand you should "learn from your mistakes" and always fold when getting that hand.  You think about it a bit more and it makes sense: life is like a partial information game, played over many rounds.  You can choose not to take risks and constantly fold, but then you will be steadily loosing chips.  Huh, you would get back into poker but online ones suck: they are rigged.  Then you remember there are cryptographic algorithms to ensure that fair games of poker can be played between untrusting parties.  They require a lot of computation however so not good as a mobile app; would have been a nice idea to make an app.  Would people play it though?  Also you don't know that much Android programming so it won't be a pretty app.  Plus it will drain the battery and people don't actually care about all the crypto algorithms.  Maybe there are some PC implementations?  How would an AI be trained on poker?  You know there are people working on this at the moment.  How would you train an AI to play poker?  Would it be self-play?  How would the policy work?  Would it be like a Monte-Carlo tree search?  Maybe how Deep Mind's Alpha Go worked?  It's interesting because in poker you can in theory make the optimum decision.  You just need to to the number crunching.  Is that applicable to life as well?  Maybe, byt life maybe has a lot more variables adn then it becomes difficul to number-crunch....
