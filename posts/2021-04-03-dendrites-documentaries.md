Bellow are "back of the envelope" numbers and stats.  They aren't high accuracy,
but hopefully are in the right ball-park.

* Oceans absorbe emissions
  * [yearly carbon emmisions](https://www.co2.earth/global-co2-emissions) are
    around ~36 Gt (1 Gt = 1 gigatonne = 1 billion tonnes = 1e9
    tonnes) of CO2.  This is equivalent to ~10 of pure carbon.
  * Carbon Brief [study](https://www.carbonbrief.org/guest-post-the-oceans-are-absorbing-more-carbon-than-previously-thought)
    says around 3Bn tonnes CO2 / year are absorbed by oceans.  This means less
    than 10%.
  * NOAA [article](https://sos.noaa.gov/datasets/ocean-atmosphere-co2-exchange/)
    says oceans absorb around 25% of emissions
* A significant portion of plastics (at least 20%) in oceans are from fishing
  activities.
  * [Ourworldindata.org](https://ourworldindata.org/faq-on-plastics#i-ve-heard-most-of-our-ocean-plastic-debris-comes-from-fishing-activity-is-this-true)
  * Guardian [article](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2019/nov/06/dumped-fishing-gear-is-biggest-plastic-polluter-in-ocean-finds-report)
  * Seasheperd [article](https://www.seashepherdglobal.org/latest-news/marine-debris-plastic-fishing-gear/).
  Seashepherd is mentioned in the documentary
* Fish are ingest the garbage we throw in oceans
  * Microplastics
  * Mercury
  * Many other harmful chemicals
  * These might outweight the benefits of things like Omega-3 acids.
* [Bycatch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bycatch)
  * Impossible to catch just the desired species
  * Bycatch includes [turtles, dolphins and others](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cetacean_bycatch)
* Sustainable fishing isn't that much better
  * Very difficult to enforce - requires observers on ships, which sometimes go
   ["missing at sea"](https://www.qwant.com/?q=fishing ship observer missing)
  * Sustainable means there's no decrease.  It doesn't mean "increase".
  * It's a label that costs money.  The incentives are for it to sell and not be
    respected.
* Fish farming is like any type of farming: cramped, filthy, feeds the animals
  mostly sh\*t and barely keeps them alive.  Would you really want to eat that?
* Farmed salmon isn't pink: it's dyed
  * The atlantic [article](https://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2015/03/the-costliest-part-of-feeding-farmed-salmon-a-pill-that-turns-them-pink/387586/)
  * Time [article](https://time.com/4790794/farmed-salmon-pink/)
  * Quartz [article](https://qz.com/358811/heres-why-your-farmed-salmon-has-color-added-to-it/)
